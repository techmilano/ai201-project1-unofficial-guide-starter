Safety in UCF Housing

UCF Housing provides a safe environment for students living on their own for the first time. Each residence hall has controlled building access and secure entry points and is staffed 24 hours a day by trained Residence Life professionals, with campus police offering continuous security and support. Staff are often among the first to respond to student concerns.

ON-CAMPUS SECURITY — SAFETY STEPS
- Never walk alone at night and do not let friends walk alone.
- Avoid dimly lit or unlit areas on campus.
- Never prop open outside doors of your residence hall.
- Report unescorted or unfamiliar persons to staff immediately.
- Be familiar with the blue-light emergency phones and use them.
- Call 407-823-5555 (UCFPD non-emergency) or 911 for any emergency.
- Always lock the door to your residence hall room.
- Inform your RA of any crisis, theft, or emergency.
- Report anything out of the ordinary to your community office.

Safety resources: Resident Fire Safety (ehs.ucf.edu/fire-safety-guidelines), UCF Police Department (police.ucf.edu), UCF Environmental Health & Safety (ehs.ucf.edu), UCF Emergency Management, UCF Facilities & Safety, UCF Victim Services (victimservices.ucf.edu).

KEYS AND LOCKOUTS
- You receive your room key at check-in and are responsible for it the entire academic year; keep it with you at all times.
- If you lose your key, a new lock core and keys are ordered and your account is billed for the new lock core.
- If locked out, bring your UCF ID to your community office for assistance.
- Residence hall staff will NOT open another resident's room for you at any time.

BIKES
Bikes are not permitted inside residence halls. Register bikes with UCF Parking Services and obtain a permit for campus use. Bike racks/storage are available in all communities; lock bikes at all times to prevent theft.

RENTERS INSURANCE
UCF strongly encourages renter's insurance. UCF and DHRL are NOT responsible for damage or loss of belongings from events such as fire, sprinkler discharge, or hurricanes (see Section 11: Limit of Liability in the housing agreement). Students should secure their own personal/property loss insurance.

HURRICANE PREP AND EVACUATION
Monitor emergency updates on the UCF website and follow Housing staff instructions. Students who can safely return home or relocate off-campus should do so before a storm. All residents must complete the Weather Emergency Plan form in the Housing Portal (myUCF > Student Self Service > Housing > Housing Portal). If Ride-Out locations are designated, students must leave their residence and report to the assigned location (UCF ID required for check-in).
- What to bring: clothing/bedding (sleeping bag or blankets), toiletries and prescription medications, important documents, non-perishable food for at least three days, flashlight/batteries/portable charger, weather radio, personal items for entertainment.
- Not allowed: pets; alcohol, tobacco, or controlled substances; weapons.
Contact your RA or community office with questions.
