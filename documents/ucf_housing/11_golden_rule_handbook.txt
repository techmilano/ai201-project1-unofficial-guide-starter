The Golden Rule Student Handbook

WHAT IT IS
The Golden Rule is a compilation of policies and procedures from different university areas that defines the rights and responsibilities of UCF students. It is published online annually and may be revised at any time; revisions supersede previously published information. It is maintained by the Office of Student Conduct and Academic Integrity (SCAI) within the Division of Student Success and Well-Being (SSWB). Contact: 407-823-4638, scai@ucf.edu, http://scai.sswb.ucf.edu. It applies to all undergraduate, graduate, and professional students. The UCF Creed (Integrity, Scholarship, Community, Creativity, Excellence) frames the document. The Community Living Guide and Golden Rule are both incorporated into the Housing Agreement.

STUDENT RIGHTS (UCF-5.006) — summary
On enrollment, students are entitled (when exercised per University procedures and without disruption) to: participation in Student Government; membership in Registered Student Organizations; freedom of expression; freedom to hold public forums; freedom to hear, write, distribute, and act on a variety of thoughts and beliefs (with limits on solicitation and on distributing materials to captive audiences such as classrooms or residential units without permission); peaceful assembly; fair and impartial proceedings in disciplinary matters; confidentiality of student records; and accommodations for students with disabilities (via Student Accessibility Services).

STUDENT RESPONSIBILITIES
By enrolling, a student consents to University, Florida Board of Governors, and State of Florida policies/laws, and is responsible for knowing and abiding by University rules.

KEY DEFINITIONS (selected)
- Consent: an understandable exchange of affirmative words or actions indicating willingness to participate in mutually agreed sexual activity; must be informed and freely/actively given; cannot be obtained by force, threat, coercion, fear, intimidation, position of influence, or a person's incapacity; silence or lack of resistance is not consent; can be withdrawn at any time. An incapacitated person (e.g., by alcohol/drugs, sleep, unconsciousness) cannot consent. Being impaired is no defense to a violation.
- Preponderance of Evidence: the "more probable than not" standard used in all disciplinary cases.
- Hold: a negative service indicator that can block registration changes, transcript access, and re-enrollment.
- Responsible Person: non-confidential university employees (incl. faculty, staff, RAs, and graduate students with authority) who have reporting obligations.
- Student / University Premises / University Official / Title IX / Clery Act / VAWA / Complainant / Respondent: defined in the handbook (standard regulatory definitions).

SMOKING (UCF-5.006(4))
Students must uphold the smoke-free campus policy (http://smokefree.sswb.ucf.edu/). Smoking of any kind is prohibited in all facilities and on University premises.

MEDICAL EMERGENCIES / AMNESTY (UCF-5.006(5); detailed in 5.007 and 5.011)
UCF strongly encourages students and Registered Student Organizations to call for medical assistance whenever someone experiences severe intoxication/impairment or serious injury after consuming alcohol and/or drugs. Recognizing students may hesitate due to potential law-enforcement or conduct involvement, the University urges calling emergency services; the primary goal is student safety. Specific amnesty procedures are in sections 5.007 (individuals) and 5.011 (organizations).

SEX-BASED MISCONDUCT & TITLE IX (UCF-5.006(6)-(7))
Non-Title IX sex-based misconduct is investigated by the Office of Nondiscrimination & Accommodations Compliance (ONAC) and adjudicated by SCAI using the preponderance standard. Both Complainant and Respondent have rights including: an advisor/support person (who may advise but not testify), remedial/protective measures, equal opportunity to present witnesses/information, impartial investigators/hearing officers (with a chance to challenge impartiality), concurrent written notice of outcomes, the right to appeal, periodic status updates, and reasonable disability accommodations. Title IX Sexual Harassment is governed by UCF Policy 2-012.

INVOLUNTARY WITHDRAWAL (UCF-5.0065)
The University may involuntarily withdraw a student who poses a "Direct Threat" — a Significant Risk (high probability of harm, threats of harm, or substantial disruption) to the health/safety of others, property, or University operations that cannot be mitigated by reasonable modifications. The procedure is applied non-discriminatorily and based on conduct, not on knowledge/belief of a disability. An Interim Involuntary Withdrawal may be imposed by the Dean of Students or designee based on reasonably available information; the student receives written notice and an opportunity to present information to the University Crisis Team within three business days. A Mandated Assessment (by a licensed professional) evaluates risk of harm to self or others.

CODE OF CONDUCT (UCF-5.008) — scope
Defines prohibited conduct (academic and non-academic), applies on and off campus as specified, and feeds the Student Conduct Review Process (5.009), sanctions, and appeals (5.010). Section 10 of the Code addresses controlled substances (referenced by the Community Living Guide).
