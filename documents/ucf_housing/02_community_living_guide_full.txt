WELCOME HOME, UCF KNIGHTS

Living in a UCF residence hall or apartment gives you the opportunity to get involved in a community and enjoy many exciting experiences. The Housing and Residence Life staff is here to assist you, but recommends residents take the lead by participating, listening, sharing knowledge, adhering to community expectations, and being a role model.

MISSION: To provide residents with safe, engaging housing communities that foster student success through innovative living and learning opportunities.
VISION: To be the premier experience for residents to live, learn, and become tomorrow's citizens.

THE UCF CREED: Integrity, Scholarship, Community, Creativity, Excellence.

DEPARTMENT OF HOUSING AND RESIDENCE LIFE (DHRL)
DHRL is comprised of 450+ student staff and full-time employees serving a residential population of approximately 7,500 students across 12 communities. Staffed mail centers exist on-campus (Apollo/Libra, Academic Village, Lake Claire, West Plaza) and off-campus (Rosen, Northview, UnionWest). The Housing Lockshop programs Persona cards/student IDs for room access and is located in the Main Campus Housing Services Building, Neptune 159, (407) 823-1239.

Staff roles include: Resident Assistant (RA) — community facilitator who answers questions, plans programs, and enforces policy; Office Assistant (OA) and Office Support Assistant (OSA) — front-desk operations, lockouts, work orders; Graduate Coordinator; Coordinator and Assistant Director, Residence Life and Education.

PARKING (residential)
Main campus parking for residential students is managed by UCF Parking Services (https://parking.ucf.edu/). All motorized vehicles must be registered online at parking.ucf.edu before picking up a decal at UCF Parking Services in Garage B. Parking differs by community. Northview residents must contact the Northview Office about a parking pass; improper parking at Northview is subject to a $30 fine.

HEALTH, SAFETY AND ACCESSIBILITY
The safety of students is a top priority; staff is available 24 hours a day. Residents are expected to report unacceptable behavior to Housing & Residence Life staff and/or the UCF or Orlando Police Department. Once a person is reported for an alleged crime or policy violation, anonymity cannot be guaranteed, as all allegations are confronted and the accused resident has the right to full disclosure.

Safety features include: staffed desks throughout day/evening; strategically placed video cameras; fire and life-safety equipment; scheduled staff rounds after hours, weekends, and holidays; 24-hour staff access; and the visitation/guest policy.

Safety tips: Never walk alone at night and don't let friends walk alone. Avoid dimly lit or unlit areas. Never prop open outside doors. Report unescorted or unfamiliar persons to staff immediately. Use the blue-light emergency phones. Call 911 for any emergency. Always lock your door and secure valuables. "See Something, Say Something."

UCF POLICE DEPARTMENT (UCFPD): Established 1973; ~75 sworn officers serving over 67,000 students and 11,000 employees. Non-emergency: (407) 823-5555. Emergency: 911.

FIRE SAFETY & COOKING: Residents must abide by state and federal fire safety laws; violations can lead to criminal prosecution and removal from housing. Cooking guidance: turn on the vent above the stove, never leave food unattended, check the oven before use, turn off appliances when done, follow microwave/food-prep instructions. If a smoke detector triggers with no fire: call the community office, identify yourself/location, turn on vent/fan and open windows, do not prop the apartment door (smoke may set off the building alarm and result in a charge).

SMOKE-FREE CAMPUS: UCF is smoke-free for faculty, staff, students, and visitors, including medicinal products.

COMMUNITY LIVING GUIDE REGULATIONS (summary of numbered policies)
Residents found in violation may face educational or punitive sanctioning, including administrative cancellation of the housing contract. DHRL may change or add policies during the year.

1. ALCOHOL — Underage (under 21) possession/consumption prohibited; manufacturing/selling prohibited; providing alcohol to under-21 prohibited; possession of alcohol in a shared space with anyone under 21 prohibited (incl. empty containers); common-source containers (kegs, party balls) prohibited; alcohol paraphernalia for overconsumption prohibited; 21+ residents may not consume in the presence of those under 21. Staff will ask violators to dispose of items; anyone present in a space containing alcohol may be found in violation.

2. ANIMALS & PETS — All animals except fish are prohibited. Aggressive/venomous fish prohibited. One fish tank under 10 gallons per resident. Feeding/touching/harming campus wildlife prohibited. Service/Emotional Support Animals require prior approval from Student Accessibility Services (SAS) and registration with Housing. Violations result in contract cancellation and referral to the CLG process.

3. BEDS — Residents may not raise, loft, or alter beds (no concrete blocks, risers, platforms). Communities with adjustable Twin XL beds: Hercules, Nike, Neptune, Apollo, Libra, Lake Claire, Rosen. Northview and Towers do NOT have adjustable beds. Labor charges apply for reassembly of altered beds.

4. BICYCLES — Bicycles may not be stored inside residential communities and may only be locked to university-approved bike racks. Register bikes at https://police.ucf.edu/property-registration.

5. COMPUTER USAGE — Personal wireless connections (routers, tethering, splitters) prohibited. All technology must be registered with UCF IT for UCF wifi.

6. COMMUNITY DISRUPTION — Noise interfering with study/sleep prohibited (pounding, amplified sound, instruments, subwoofer bass). Quiet hours: 10:00 p.m.–8:00 a.m. during the academic year, and 24 hours during finals. Noxious odors (cigarette, marijuana, perfume, air freshener, dirty laundry, etc.) prohibited. Tampering with hall resources prohibited.

7. CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES — Illegal drugs and controlled substances prohibited; medical marijuana is NOT allowed in UCF owned/managed/affiliated halls; misused prescription/non-prescription drugs prohibited; drug paraphernalia prohibited; being in the presence of drugs/paraphernalia prohibited. Violations may result in agreement termination; students pay termination fees plus the remaining term's rent and must vacate within 72 hours.

8. DISRUPTIVE CONDUCT — Behavior adversely impacting residents/communities prohibited.

9. ELECTRICAL & APPLIANCES — Unapproved electrical devices prohibited; octopus/torchiere/halogen lamps prohibited; exposed-element appliances prohibited outside kitchens; appliances over 1,000 watts prohibited; refrigerators over 5 cubic feet prohibited; scented plug-ins prohibited; 3-D printers prohibited; induction devices prohibited. Surge protectors/extension cords must be UL-grounded three-prong with internal circuit breaker, max six outlets, no damage, load under 20 amps, not under carpet/furniture. LED strip lights with adhesive backs not permitted. String lights allowed around windows only, away from flammables.

10. FAILURE TO COMPLY — Non-compliance with staff/official instructions prohibited.
11. FAILURE TO REPORT — Failing to report CLG/policy violations is prohibited; students present during a violation may be held responsible.
12. FIRE SAFETY — No starting fires, false alarms, or tampering with safety equipment; must evacuate during alarms (Florida Statute 633.202); no explosives, flammables, open flames (candles/incense), live trees, space heaters; no splicing electrical wiring; wall decorations may not cover more than 20% of a wall; no curtains as partitions; nothing affixed to ceilings that could cause fire or block sprinklers. Closet curtains via tension rod must sit 20 inches below the ceiling for sprinkler coverage.
13. GAMBLING — Prohibited in all residential communities.
14. GAMES & SPORTS — Physical recreation indoors (scooters, soccer, football, skateboarding, rollerblading, basketball) prohibited; activities threatening property prohibited; fishing prohibited on UCF property.
15. HAZING — Prohibited.
16. HARMFUL BEHAVIOR — Physical harm, threats, harassment, bullying, verbal/written abuse, or retaliation prohibited.
17. HOUSEKEEPING — Must maintain a clean space; remove trash weekly; store open food in containers; no trash/personal items in shared areas; cannot refuse custodial/maintenance services. Fees apply for cleaning/facility issues; annual communities may incur "Welcome Ready" fines.
18. IDENTIFICATION — Must present valid UCF/government ID on request; no lending/borrowing IDs; no false ID info; persons without ID treated as trespassing.
19. ILLEGAL ENTRY & TRESPASSING — No unauthorized entry to living/restricted areas; no admitting unknown persons; no propping/disabling locks; no entry when communities are closed; no roof access.
20. KEYS — No unauthorized use/duplication; no switching/borrowing; report lost keys immediately; return mail keys; only hand keys to DHRL staff at checkout.
21. PUBLIC AREAS — Common areas/corridors must stay clear of obstructions, personal property, and trash.
22. RECORDING OTHERS — Recording/distributing another's image or voice without permission where privacy is expected is prohibited.
23. ROOMMATE AGREEMENT — Violating the roommate agreement is prohibited; revisit changes with your RA.
24. ROOM CHANGES — No changing assignments/moving without preapproval from the community Coordinator/office; follow the room-change process; unauthorized changes risk conduct violations.
25. ROOM USE PROHIBITIONS — No permanent/semi-permanent alterations (painting, removing/stacking furniture); no subletting; no on-site businesses; no use of vacant spaces/furniture; no relocating common-area furniture; no mounting items to walls/furniture; no decorating common areas without approval; no shower caddies hung from shower heads. Charges assessed to return space to move-in-ready condition; a vacancy may be filled by a new roommate at any time.
26. SEXUAL MISCONDUCT — All forms prohibited; subject to law enforcement, conduct violations, and agreement termination under Title IX. Sexual activity/masturbation in the presence of others is a CLG violation even if not a Title IX violation.
27. SMOKING — All smoking, including e-cigarettes/juuls/inhalant pens, prohibited inside hall entryways; UCF is smoke-free (https://smokefree.sswb.ucf.edu/), including medicinal products.
28. SOLICITATION — Soliciting and unapproved flyers/postings/chalk art prohibited.
29. TRANSPORTATION — Unauthorized motorized vehicles outside designated zones prohibited; no vehicle maintenance in communities; non-motorized transport (skateboards, scooters, etc.) may not be used in facilities; hoverboards/boosted boards/self-balancing scooters may not be charged/stored/used in or around UCF communities. See https://parking.ucf.edu/electric-scooters/.
30. VANDALISM & THEFT — Willful destruction, defacement, or theft of property prohibited.
31. VISITATION & GUESTS —
   A. Unescorted guests prohibited.
   B. Overnight guests for more than three consecutive nights in a semester prohibited (overnight = any guest past midnight).
   C. Overnight guests for more than seven nights total in a semester prohibited.
   D. Each resident may have up to two guests at a time (e.g., a triple has a max occupancy of nine: three residents + six guests).
   E. Must give roommates adequate notice of overnight guests — 48 hours unless the Roommate Agreement says otherwise.
   F. Cohabitation by anyone not assigned to the room prohibited.
   G. Gatherings exceeding designated room/apartment occupancy prohibited.
   H. Residents are personally and financially responsible for their guests' behavior and policy violations. A roommate's rights to privacy, sleep, and study take precedence over a host's right to have a guest.
32. WEAPONS — Possession/storage/manufacture/use of weapons or munitions prohibited; using items to cause fear/harm prohibited; kitchen knives only for cooking in kitchens (chef's knives kept in a soft carrier/block); no shipping weapons to a resident PO Box. Prohibited weapons include guns (BB/pellet, nerf, water, airsoft, paintball), sporting/hunting, edged (knives, swords), blunt (martial arts, nightstick), pocket knives over 2 inches, tasers, stun guns. A self-defense chemical spray up to 2 ounces is allowed.
33. WINDOWS & SCREENS — No obscuring/covering/blocking windows; nothing thrown/hung from windows; windows not used as entry/exit except in emergencies; no tampering with/removing screens or latches. Windows must remain closed and locked except in an emergency or to ventilate smoke.

STUDENT SUPPORT
Student Care Services (SCS) supports students facing academic, financial, or personal challenges and partners with Housing: https://scs.sswb.ucf.edu/
Title IX: UCF prohibits sex discrimination, sexual harassment, and sexual violence; report to the Title IX Coordinator or any Housing staff. Resources: https://letsbeclear.ucf.edu/

CONDUCT PROCESS (CLG flowchart, summarized): An alleged violation generates an Incident Report (IR) → reviewed and assigned to a hearing officer → hearing officer determines whether a conduct meeting is needed → a CLG Violation Meeting is scheduled and a letter is sent → the hearing officer meets with those involved (a decision can be made without a student's input if they do not appear) → if found responsible, a warning letter may be issued (no response required) and the case is closed; other outcomes follow the appeals process described in the CLG.
