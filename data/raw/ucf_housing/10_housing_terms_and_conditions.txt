UCF 2025-2026 Housing Agreement — Terms & Conditions

PARTIES & NATURE
A legally binding agreement between the UCF Board of Trustees ("University") and an admitted/enrolled student ("Student"). Submitting the agreement online (or delivering a signed hard copy) expresses the student's desire to occupy an assigned space; DHRL assigns the specific facility and room at its discretion. The agreement is effective immediately upon electronic signature/submission.

ARTICLE I — GENERAL
- Eligibility (1.4): Student must be admitted to the University and submit the agreement plus prepaid rent (unless waived). While in residence, a student must be enrolled in and attending 12+ undergraduate or 6+ graduate credit hours in fall/spring, and/or 3+ credit hours in summer. If enrollment drops below these levels but is at least one credit hour, the student must apply in writing (Credit Hour Exemption Form) within 15 days. If a student drops to 0 credit hours, they must move out within 72 hours. The summer enrollment requirement is waived for continuing residents in Towers, NorthView, Rosen, and Greek communities.
- Term (1.5): Move-out must occur 24 hours after the student's last final exam or the academic-calendar move-out date, whichever is first. Early move-in is not guaranteed and may incur fees.
- Occupancy (1.6): Begins when the student signs for keys and ends when keys are returned per move-out procedures. A student may not designate another person to pick up/turn in keys.
- Non-transferable (1.7): Only the assigned student may occupy the space; no subletting, sharing, dividing, or transferring. Residents may not remove or add beds.
- Guest and visitor access (1.8): May be prohibited or restricted within the space or building. The student is responsible for guests' actions and damages. A student may not admit anyone they know has been evicted from housing or banned by a University authority.
- Authorized entry (1.9): Authorized UCF personnel may enter facilities in emergencies and for occupancy checks, lockouts, inspections (health/safety/maintenance/compliance), maintenance, and fire-code enforcement, or if they reasonably believe a violation/law-breaking is in progress. Personal property is not searched without consent except by law enforcement under law.
- Care and maintenance (1.10): Student must keep the space clean and sanitary, control pests, and not move assigned furniture. Florida's climate is conducive to mold/mildew; the student must properly ventilate and dehumidify per the Community Living Guide's Air Quality guidance.
- Space assignment (1.12): UCF assigns without regard to race, color, religion, national origin, age, and/or sexual orientation. DHRL does NOT guarantee a specific community, building, room, roommate, room type, or occupancy, and may consolidate or change assignments for order, health, safety, security, utilization, or nonpayment. Students who refuse consolidation may be charged additional rent.
- Accessibility (1.15): Accommodation requests are submitted via the Housing Portal during the application process; DHRL may confer with Student Accessibility Services and/or Student Health Services. Information is kept confidential.
- Utilities (1.16): DHRL is not liable for utility interruptions; the University may assess additional charges with 30 days' written notice.
- Damage (1.17): Students are responsible for damage they cause; shared-area damage with no identified responsible person is the joint responsibility of all sharing the space.
- Adherence to policies (1.20): The DHRL Community Living Guide (CLG) and the UCF Golden Rule are incorporated into the agreement.
- Health and safety (1.21): Student acknowledges inherent risk of community living; UCF does not carry insurance for a student's illness/injury. Students must comply with pandemic-related policies (social distancing, symptom checking/testing, quarantine/isolation) and may be relocated; pandemic declarations may trigger guest/visitor restrictions.
- Items left at departure (1.22): Belongings left after move-out (or 24+ hours after an unscheduled move-out) are deemed abandoned; DHRL may discard them, rekey locks, and charge the student.

ARTICLE II — RENT AND PAYMENT
- Rates (2.1): Posted at housing.ucf.edu/costs/rates/ and subject to change. Financial aid is applied to housing charges before any reimbursement; the agreement is not contingent on aid.
- Rental prepayment (2.2): $250.00 for agreements beginning in spring or fall; $100.00 for summer. Prepayments are refunded only if DHRL cannot offer housing, for cancellations before set deadlines (Exhibit A), or at DHRL discretion.
- Payment (2.3): Rent is due by the dates on the Academic Calendar; DHRL does not send invoices — the student must monitor their account at my.ucf.edu. Students assigned after the due date get an automatic 14-day extension.
- Taxes (2.5): Agreements under six months + one day, offered to non-full-time students, may be subject to ~12.5% Florida Sales/Use Tax and Orange County Tourist Development Tax.
- Default (2.6): Late fee up to $100 once per semester for overdue balances. DHRL may place a HOLD (blocking enrollment, registration, grades, diploma, transcripts), temporarily restrict residence access, and refer balances to collections (non-appealable).
- Florida Prepaid (2.7): FPCDP covers the cost of a standard air-conditioned double room only; the student pays differences and any non-rental charges (e.g., lost keys, cleaning).

ARTICLE III — CANCELLATION AND TERMINATION
- Cancellation vs. termination (3.1): Cancellation can trigger additional rents/cancellation fees; termination is completion of or early release from the agreement and does not entitle DHRL to additional rents. Fees already charged remain due.
- Administrative cancellation (3.2): DHRL may cancel if a student is suspended/expelled, fails obligations (incl. timely payment or perfecting financial aid), is sanctioned for policy violations, or has unresolved matters from a prior agreement. DHRL may also relocate/terminate for order, health, discipline, disaster, or utilization.
- Cancellation fees (3.3-3.4): Charged when the agreement is cancelled; calculated by agreement duration, the date cancellation is received, and the date move-out completes (Exhibit A schedule). Due upon submission of the cancellation request.
- Procedure (3.5): All cancellation requests must be IN WRITING via the address in 1.19, the Housing Portal, or housing@ucf.edu.
- Effect of student cancellation (3.6): The student may remain through the end of the semester the notice was given (if still eligible). The student is always responsible for the greater of the pro-rata rent or the cancellation fee for that semester. Cancelling forfeits any current/prior-resident preference for future applications.
- Summer portion (3.7): A student may not cancel only the third (summer) semester of a three-semester agreement during the fall without cancelling the spring portion too.
- Student terminations (3.8) — allowed with documentation for: Graduation; Academic Disqualification; Military Deployment (partial refund); Medical Withdrawal approved by Academic Services (partial refund; non-withdrawal medical conditions do not qualify); Internship/exchange/co-op that awards UCF credit, prevents residing for 3+ weeks, and is 50+ miles from campus and the residence; Involuntary Withdrawal (partial refund); and other reasons at the discretion of the Assistant Vice President for Housing for the student's safety/well-being.

ARTICLE IV — SANCTIONS AND APPEALS
- Non-appealable bases (4.1): Financial inconvenience/changes in financial circumstance, and lack of classes located conveniently to the residence (including online or other-campus offerings), are NOT valid bases for termination.
- Sanctions (4.2): May include administrative warnings, monetary sanctions, referral to Student Conduct, and administrative cancellation with cancellation fees; multiple departments/boards may impose additional sanctions.
- Service fees (4.3): Charges are posted at housing.ucf.edu/costs/charges/.

ARTICLE V — MISCELLANEOUS
- Entire agreement (5.1): Written agreement supersedes oral understandings; amendments require a written instrument executed by DHRL.
- University interpretation controls (5.2).
- FERPA (5.3): DHRL may release personally identifiable information consistent with FERPA.
