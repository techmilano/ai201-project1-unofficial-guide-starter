Open Housing

UCF Housing and Residence Life offers an Open Housing option to students of all classifications and in all housing agreement types. Open Housing allows students to reside together in the same room, suite, or apartment, regardless of sex. When students complete a housing application, they are given the opportunity to opt in to Open Housing. Students who do not opt in will roommate match and room select with students who have the same sex listed on their myUCF.

OPEN HOUSING GUIDELINES
- Any student may opt in to Open Housing when completing a housing agreement.
- Students opting in can only roommate match with other students of the same housing agreement type who have also opted in.
- Students will not be automatically placed in an Open Housing suite/apartment unless they indicated on their agreement that they'd be comfortable living in Open Housing.
- Students must be able to fill all spaces in the suite/apartment with other students who requested Open Housing during room selection.
- Open Housing is the student's decision and is never forced on an individual. Vacancies are filled by another individual opting in to Open Housing if possible.
- UCF DHRL may initiate consolidation procedures to prevent vacant Open Housing spaces; affected students are notified by email.
- UCF DHRL encourages students to discuss the program with parents/guardians beforehand.
- The program is NOT designed for romantic couples; students in intimate relationships should not choose to live together in Open Housing.
- Requesting Open Housing does not guarantee a specific room type or building; room selection is based on availability.
- Students who opted in may opt out during the Roommate Matching timeframe in the Housing Portal.
